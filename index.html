Historical Context

Rome's history spans over 2,500 years as the center of the Roman Empire. The sites you'll visit, like the Colosseum, date back to its height (approx. 1st century BCE to 4th century CE). Trastevere, a historic working-class district, has maintained much of its charming medieval character.

Day 1: Arrival & Food Tour

Arrive in Rome, check into Hotel Baglioni Regina, and enjoy an evening food tour in Trastevere. Highlights include sampling authentic street food and a multi-course meal with wine.

Day 2: Ancient Rome

A guided tour of the Colosseum and Ancient City, exploring the heart of the Roman Empire.

Day 3: Cooking Class

A hands-on Pasta & Tiramisu cooking class to learn the secrets of authentic Italian cuisine.

Day 4: Vatican Treasures

Discover the vast art collections of the Vatican Museums. Remember to dress modestly!

Historical Context

Sorrento has been a popular resort since Roman emperors built villas here. The Amalfi Coast was a powerful maritime republic in the medieval period. Pompeii offers an unparalleled glimpse into daily Roman life, preserved under volcanic ash from the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 CE.

Day 5: Travel & Pompeii

Travel to Sorrento via high-speed train, stopping for a guided tour of Pompeii's remarkably preserved ruins.

Day 6: Amalfi Coast Tour

Explore the breathtaking scenery and picturesque towns of the world-famous Amalfi Coast.

Day 7: Capri & Anacapri

Take a memorable tour of the enchanting and glamorous island of Capri and its quieter counterpart, Anacapri.

Day 8: Departure

Transfer to Naples Airport for your return flight, filled with unforgettable memories. Arrivederci!

ðŸ“œ Cultural Etiquette

  • Pace of Life: Embrace a more relaxed pace. Service at restaurants may be slower as meals are meant to be savored, not rushed.
  • Dining Customs: Italians dine later than Americans (lunch ~1-2 PM, dinner 8 PM+). Cappuccino is a morning drink, never after a big meal!
  • Greetings: A simple "Buongiorno" (Good day) and "Grazie" (Thank you) are much appreciated.
  • Church Etiquette: Dress modestly when visiting churches, with shoulders and knees covered. Be respectful and maintain a quiet voice.

  • Comfortable Shoes: Essential! You'll walk a lot on uneven cobblestone streets.
  • Safety: Be aware of your surroundings and watch out for pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.
