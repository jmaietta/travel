Tipping Heuristics

  • Restaurants: Not required, but rounding up or leaving a few euros for great service is appreciated. A 5-10% tip is very generous. Check for a *servizio incluso* (service charge) on the bill.
  • Taxis: Not expected, but rounding up to the nearest euro is a kind gesture.

ðŸ“‹ General Advice

  • Comfortable Shoes: Essential! You'll walk a lot on uneven cobblestone streets.
  • Safety: Be aware of your surroundings and watch out for pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.
  • Electronics: Italy uses the Type F (2-prong) plug at 230V. Bring a universal adapter.
  • Pace of Life: Embrace a more relaxed pace. Meals are meant to be savored.
  • Language Basics: Locals appreciate it when you try a few Italian phrases like *Buongiorno* (Good day), *Per favore* (Please), and *Grazie* (Thank you).
  • Modest Dress: Have a scarf or jacket handy to cover shoulders and knees when entering churches.
